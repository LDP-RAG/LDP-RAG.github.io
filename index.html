Abstract

Retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) improves factuality by conditioning on retrieved passages, but it also creates a new privacy attack surface: any sensitive snippet fetched by the retriever can be reproduced by the generator and leaked to the user.

VAGUE-Gate is a portable, training-free local-privacy gate that sits in front of any RAG system. Given a privacy budget Œµ, it first performs a precision pass to drop low-utility tokens, then applies a small number of high-temperature paraphrase passes that obscure residual cues, all while preserving an Œµ-local differential privacy guarantee.

Across eight RAG pipelines and four state-of-the-art LLMs, VAGUE-Gate at Œµ=0.3 cuts lexical leakage by around 70 % and reduces semantic leakage, while retaining roughly 90 % of the faithfulness of a plain RAG baseline and adding only ~240 ms of latency.

Method

High-level idea

VAGUE-Gate runs entirely on the data-holder side. It rewrites each retrieved chunk before the LLM sees it, so private atoms are protected even if the generator is untrusted or logs prompts verbatim.

  • Semantic adaptive chunking with sentence-level privacy labels (Public / Sensitive / Confidential).
  • A precision pass at temperature 0 that drops or generalises atomic facts under an Œµ-aware rule.
  • A small number of higher-temperature ‚Äúdeep obfuscation‚Äù passes that increase vagueness without exceeding the original privacy budget (post-processing property of DP).
Theory

Privacy guarantee

The gate is proven Œµ-locally differentially private: for any two inputs differing in one atomic fact, the probability of any output differs by at most a factor of exp(Œµ).

Only the precision pass is randomised; the paraphrase stages are pure post-processing, and therefore cannot increase privacy loss. This yields a controllable privacy‚Äìutility continuum via the choice of Œµ and the repetition schedule k(Œµ).

Results

Empirical findings

  • Benchmarked on a 10k-question PrivRAG/BlendPriv suite spanning customer service, healthcare, and legal domains.
  • Compared against eight strong baselines: plain, hybrid, and hierarchical RAG, entity-perturbation, and several prompt-level obfuscators.
  • Evaluated on faithfulness, answer relevancy, ROUGE-L, BLEU-4, and two new leakage metrics: a cold-stats Information-Leakage Score and an LLM-as-judge score.
Implementation

Reproducing the gate

Core ingredients for a minimal implementation:

  • Sentence-level privacy labels or a classifier to decide which spans to treat as private.
  • Two prompt templates: one deterministic precision prompt and one vaguer paraphrase prompt.
  • A simple scheduler mapping privacy budget Œµ to number of paraphrase passes k(Œµ).

The official implementation (linked above) includes full prompts, hyperparameters, and evaluation scripts.
